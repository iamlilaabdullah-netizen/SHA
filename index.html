Our Mission

We advance policies and initiatives that promote affordable, safe, and accessible housing options for all MSU students—on and off campus. We educate our community on critical housing issues and empower students to organize and advocate effectively.

  • Advocacy with university and local government
  • Policy research & evidence addenda
  • Know-your-rights education & tenant resources
  • Coalition building with student and community orgs

Current Focus Areas

  • Eliminating barriers like non-refundable housing application fees
  • Improving maintenance response standards and accountability
  • Expanding access to affordable housing and transit options

These priorities evolve with member input and ongoing research.

Constitution

Preamble

We, the students of Michigan State University, recognizing the critical importance of affordable, safe, and accessible housing for the well-being and success of the student body, establish this Constitution for the Spartan Housing Alliance in pursuit of advocacy, education, and community empowerment in housing justice.

Article I — Name and Purpose

Section 1: Name. The name of this organization shall be Spartan Housing Alliance at Michigan State University.

Section 2: Mission and Purpose. The organization shall:

  1. Advocate for policies and initiatives that promote affordable, safe, and accessible housing options for all MSU students, on and off campus.
  2. Educate students and the broader community on critical housing issues.
  3. Empower students to engage in housing-related advocacy efforts.
  4. Collaborate with university administration, government entities, community organizations, and student groups to advance our mission.
Article II — Membership

Section 1: General Membership. Any currently enrolled student at Michigan State University who expresses interest in the organization's mission may be considered a General Member.

Section 2: Active Member Status. Active Members have voting rights and eligibility to propose or lead campaigns, subject to participation criteria adopted by the organization.
